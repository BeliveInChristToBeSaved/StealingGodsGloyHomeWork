A 30-Day Journey

Brokenness & Transformation in Marriage Leadership

Daily Scripture for the man who steals God's glory through control

Week One
Seeing the Sin Clearly
You cannot be broken over what you have not named. This week is about letting God show you the full weight of what control and pride actually are.
Day 1 Isaiah 42:8 Stolen Glory
"I am the Lord; that is my name; my glory I give to no other, nor my praise to carved idols."
Meditate OnWhen you lead through fear and control, you sit on a throne that belongs only to God. The idol here is not carved from wood — it is carved from your ego. Ask: In what specific moments this week did I take His throne in my home?
PrayLord, show me every moment I have taken what is Yours. I do not want Your glory. Break me of wanting it.
Day 2 Genesis 3:16 The Curse You're Living In
"Your desire will be for your husband, and he will rule over you."
Meditate OnThis is not a command — it is a curse. Domineering rule over your wife is the mark of the fall, not the Kingdom. Every time you dictate, you announce that you are living beneath redemption. Christ came to reverse this.
PrayGod, I have been living in the curse while claiming the crown. Forgive me. Pull me out of Adam and into Christ.
Day 3 Proverbs 16:18 The Pride Root
"Pride goes before destruction, and a haughty spirit before a fall."
Meditate OnDictatorship in marriage is pride with a spiritual label on it. "I'm the head" becomes a weapon instead of a cross. Where in your leadership is pride hiding underneath the language of authority?
PrayFather, show me my haughty spirit. I do not want to fall — I want to kneel. Destroy the pride before it destroys my marriage.
Day 4 Mark 10:42–45 Kingdom vs. World
"You know that those who are considered rulers of the Gentiles lord it over them... But it shall not be so among you. Whoever would be great among you must be your servant."
Meditate OnJesus drew a line: the world's authority looks one way, the Kingdom's looks completely opposite. Which side of that line does your marriage leadership fall on today? Be honest.
PrayJesus, I have led like the Gentiles, not like You. Redefine greatness for me until I want the towel more than the throne.
Day 5 1 Corinthians 11:3 The Broken Chain
"The head of every man is Christ, the head of a wife is her husband, and the head of Christ is God."
Meditate OnA dictator is a man who demands submission from his wife while refusing it to Christ. You cannot hold authority you are not under. Where are you functionally autonomous from Christ while demanding your wife submit?
PrayLord, I have broken the chain by refusing to be under You. I re-submit my headship to You today. Lead me so I can lead.
Day 6 Proverbs 29:25 Fear Underneath Control
"The fear of man lays a snare, but whoever trusts in the Lord is safe."
Meditate OnControl is fear that has put on the clothes of strength. You control because you are afraid — of losing respect, of being irrelevant, of your wife not needing you. Name the specific fear underneath your controlling behavior.
PrayGod, I am afraid. Show me what I am afraid of. Replace my fear of man with trust in You.
Day 7 Psalm 51:1–4, 10 True Repentance
"Have mercy on me, O God... Against you, you only, have I sinned... Create in me a clean heart, O God, and renew a right spirit within me."
Meditate OnDavid did not minimize. He did not explain. He did not justify. He collapsed before God. This is the posture for the end of Week 1. Don't move to Week 2 until you have sat in this psalm for a full day.
PrayPray Psalm 51 aloud, slowly, substituting your specific sins in marriage for David's. Do not rush this.
Week Two
The Cross-Shaped Husband
Brokenness without a new vision produces despair, not transformation. This week you look at Jesus — the only model of authority that does not steal glory.
Day 8 Ephesians 5:25–27 The Giving Model
"Husbands, love your wives, as Christ loved the church and gave himself up for her, that he might sanctify her..."
Meditate OnThe purpose of headship is your wife's sanctification, not your satisfaction. Christ's love was directional — it moved toward her need, not His comfort. What is your wife's deepest spiritual need right now, and are you moving toward it?
PrayFather, shift my leadership from taking to giving. Show me specifically what my wife needs from me this week.
Day 9 Philippians 2:3–8 The Downward Move
"Do nothing from selfish ambition or conceit, but in humility count others more significant than yourselves... [Christ] emptied himself, taking the form of a servant."
Meditate OnChrist moved down. He had every right to rule — and He knelt. Kenosis (self-emptying) is the shape of true headship. What would it look like for you to empty yourself in your marriage today in one concrete act?
PrayJesus, You descended. Teach me to descend. Kill my need to be seen as the authority.
Day 10 John 13:4–5, 12–15 The Towel
"[Jesus] rose from supper... and began to wash the disciples' feet... 'If I then, your Lord and Teacher, have washed your feet, you also ought to wash one another's feet.'"
Meditate OnJesus was certain of His authority (v.3) — and that security is exactly what freed Him to serve. Insecure men dominate. Secure men kneel. Your dictatorship reveals insecurity, not strength. Let His security become yours.
PrayLord, I want to be so secure in You that I can pick up the towel without feeling diminished. Give me that security.
Day 11 1 Peter 3:7 Understanding Her
"Likewise, husbands, live with your wives in an understanding way, showing honor to the woman as the weaker vessel, since they are heirs with you of the grace of life, so that your prayers may not be hindered."
Meditate OnGod ties your prayer life directly to how you treat your wife. A dictator does not seek to understand — he seeks to be understood. "Understanding way" means study her. Know her. Do you truly know your wife's fears, wounds, and hopes?
PrayGod, show me how little I have sought to understand my wife. Give me curiosity for her. And forgive me for the prayers I have forfeited through dishonor.
Day 12 Colossians 3:19 The Prohibition
"Husbands, love your wives, and do not be harsh with them."
Meditate OnThis verse is four words in Greek — and one of them is a command not to be bitter or harsh. The word means to embitter, to exasperate. Sit with this: in what specific ways have you been harsh, cold, dismissive, or cutting toward your wife?
PrayFather, name for me every harsh word and cold silence. I repent of each one.
Day 13 1 John 4:18–19 Love Casts Out Fear
"There is no fear in love, but perfect love casts out fear... We love because he first loved us."
Meditate OnYour wife should not be afraid of you. If she walks on eggshells, goes silent, or shrinks — that is the wound of your dictatorship made visible. Love and fear cannot coexist. Which does your wife experience in your presence?
PrayLord, has my wife been afraid of me? Show me. I do not want to be a man who creates fear. Cast out every root of that in me.
Day 14 Romans 12:10 Outdo One Another
"Love one another with brotherly affection. Outdo one another in showing honor."
Meditate OnThe competitive instinct God wired in you was meant for this: a race to honor your wife. Are you competing to be served or competing to serve? End this week by asking your wife one question: "How can I honor you better?"
PrayGod, redirect my competitiveness toward honoring my wife. Let me outdo her in love — not in pride.
Week Three
Dying to Self Daily
Transformation is not an event — it is a daily death. This week is about the hard, unglamorous discipline of putting the flesh to death in real marriage moments.
Day 15 Galatians 2:20 Not I, But Christ
"I have been crucified with Christ. It is no longer I who live, but Christ who lives in me."
Meditate OnThe dictator in your marriage is the "I" that has not yet been crucified. Every controlling impulse, every demand, every cold silence is the old self refusing the cross. What specific part of "you" needs to die in your marriage today?
PrayChrist, crucify the dictator in me. I do not want to lead from my flesh anymore. Live through me toward my wife today.
Day 16 Luke 9:23 Daily Cross
"If anyone would come after me, let him deny himself and take up his cross daily and follow me."
Meditate OnThe word is daily. There is no permanent victory over the flesh — there is only today's death. What does taking up the cross look like in a Tuesday morning disagreement with your wife? In a Friday night power struggle?
PrayJesus, today I pick up the cross again. Show me where I will need to die today before I even get there.
Day 17 James 4:6–7 God Opposes the Proud
"God opposes the proud but gives grace to the humble. Submit yourselves therefore to God. Resist the devil and he will flee from you."
Meditate OnThis is sobering: God is actively opposing you when you lead in pride. He is not neutral. The transformation you want in your marriage begins with radical submission to God — not better leadership strategies.
PrayGod, I do not want You against me. I humble myself before You now. I submit. Resist the enemy who has fed my pride for so long.
Day 18 Proverbs 15:1 The Tongue in Marriage
"A soft answer turns away wrath, but a harsh word stirs up anger."
Meditate OnMuch of your dictatorship lives in your tone and words. A sharp answer, a dismissive tone, a loud silence — these are weapons. Think back: what is the tone your wife most often experiences from you? What tone do you want her to experience?
PrayLord, set a guard over my mouth. Let my wife hear something different from me today.
Day 19 Ephesians 4:31–32 Put It Off / Put It On
"Let all bitterness and wrath and anger and clamor and slander be put away from you... Be kind to one another, tenderhearted, forgiving one another, as God in Christ forgave you."
Meditate OnPaul gives two movements: put off, put on. Write down what you need to put off in your marriage. Then write what you need to put on. Post it somewhere you will see it every morning.
PrayFather, I put off [name them specifically]. I put on kindness, tenderness, and forgiveness toward my wife. I cannot do this without You.
Day 20 Matthew 5:23–24 Go First
"If you are offering your gift at the altar and there remember that your brother has something against you, leave your gift there... first go and be reconciled."
Meditate OnGod interrupts worship for reconciliation. If your wife has something against you — and after years of dictatorship, she likely does — God says: go first. Don't wait for her to come to you. That is still the dictator talking.
PrayGod, who do I need to go to first? Give me the humility to go to my wife before I come back to this altar.
Day 21 Micah 6:8 What God Requires
"He has told you, O man, what is good; and what does the Lord require of you but to do justice, and to love kindness, and to walk humbly with your God?"
Meditate OnThree things. Not spiritual success. Not leadership excellence. Justice — have you been just toward your wife? Kindness — not occasionally, but as a way of life? Humility with God — daily, not just in crisis? Score yourself honestly against all three.
PrayLord, this is all You require. And I have failed at all three. Start building these in me from the ground up.
Week Four
Walking in the New Man
Brokenness is not the destination — Christ-likeness is. This week is about the new identity you walk in as a redeemed husband and steward of God's glory.
Day 22 Ephesians 5:28–29 She Is Your Own Body
"Husbands should love their wives as their own bodies. He who loves his wife loves himself. For no one ever hated his own flesh, but nourishes and cherishes it."
Meditate OnTwo words: nourish and cherish. To nourish is to build up. To cherish is to hold warmly. Ask yourself: is my wife being built up by my presence? Does she feel warmly held by me? If not — what is one specific thing you will change?
PrayFather, let my wife be better — stronger, more secure, more joyful — because she is married to me. Make me a man who nourishes.
Day 23 Proverbs 31:28–29 Praise Her Publicly
"Her husband praises her: 'Many women have done excellently, but you surpass them all.'"
Meditate OnWhen did you last sincerely praise your wife — to her face, to your children, to others? A dictator diminishes. A servant-leader magnifies. Today, tell your wife three specific things you genuinely admire about her. Speak them out loud.
PrayLord, loosen my tongue for praise of my wife. Let me be the loudest voice calling out her worth.
Day 24 Joshua 24:15 Lead by Going First
"As for me and my house, we will serve the Lord."
Meditate OnJoshua did not say "my house will serve." He said me and my house — he went first. Spiritual leadership is not demanding that your family follow God. It is being so visibly surrendered to God that they are drawn to follow. What does your wife see you surrendering to?
PrayGod, make my surrender visible. Let my wife see a man who follows You — not a man who demands she follow him.
Day 25 Song of Solomon 2:3–4 Delight in Her
"His banner over me was love."
Meditate OnThe Song of Solomon is in your Bible because God designed marriage to include deep delight. A dictator uses his wife. A husband delights in his wife. When did you last pursue your wife with pure delight — not for what she does but for who she is? Do that today.
PrayLord, restore my delight in my wife. Let her feel pursued and cherished, not managed and endured.
Day 26 Romans 15:1–3 Bear Her Weaknesses
"We who are strong have an obligation to bear with the failings of the weak, and not to please ourselves. Let each of us please his neighbor for his good, to build him up. For Christ did not please himself."
Meditate OnStrength in marriage is not authority over weakness — it is the patient bearing of weakness. Where have you been impatient, dismissive, or contemptuous of your wife's struggles? That is strength misused. Bear with her as Christ bore with you.
PrayChrist, You bore my every failure with patience. Give me that same patience toward my wife's.
Day 27 Ephesians 4:15 Truth in Love
"Rather, speaking the truth in love, we are to grow up in every way into him who is the head, into Christ."
Meditate OnA dictator speaks truth without love (it becomes a weapon) or withholds truth for peace (it becomes cowardice). The spiritual leader holds both — truth that serves her growth, delivered in love that protects her dignity. Are you doing this?
PrayFather, teach me to speak truth that builds my wife up, never truth wielded to win arguments or prove points.
Day 28 Hebrews 13:4 + Ecclesiastes 9:9 Honor the Gift
"Let marriage be held in honor among all." / "Enjoy life with the wife whom you love, all the days of your vain life... for this is your portion in life."
Meditate OnGod calls your wife your portion — your assigned treasure. You do not deserve her. She is given. Dictators take gifts for granted. Stewards hold them with awe. Do you still hold your wife with awe? What has familiarity killed in your heart toward her?
PrayLord, restore the wonder. Let me see my wife as the gift she is — today and every day.
The Final Days
Seal It and Continue
These last two days are not an ending. They are a commissioning. The 30 days mean nothing if they don't become a new way of life.
Day 29 Lamentations 3:22–23 New Every Morning
"The steadfast love of the Lord never ceases; his mercies never come to an end; they are new every morning; great is your faithfulness."
Meditate OnYou will fail again. There will be days when the dictator rises. The mercy of God does not run out on day 30. Every morning is a new beginning — for you and for your marriage. This is not permission to sin. It is fuel to keep fighting.
PrayLord, thank You that Your mercies outlast my failures. I receive new mercy today. I will need it again tomorrow.
Day 30 Revelation 5:12 + Revelation 4:11 Give It Back
"Worthy is the Lamb who was slain, to receive power and wealth and wisdom and might and honor and glory and blessing." / "Worthy are you, our Lord and God, to receive glory and honor and power."
Meditate OnThis is where it ends — and where it begins again. You started this journey because you were stealing God's glory in your marriage. End by giving glory back to where it belongs. You are not the head of your home. Christ is. You are a steward. Lay it down at His feet.
PrayLord, I give You back the glory I have stolen. My marriage is Yours. My wife is entrusted to me by You. I will lead only as a man under Your Lordship. Worthy is the Lamb — not me. Amen.

CONTINUE THE JOURNEY

Start again on Day 1. The second time through, you will find different things. Real transformation takes years, not 30 days. Stay broken. Stay soft. Stay under Christ.
